Larry Page

Early Life

Larry Page was born on March 26, 1973, in East Lansing, Michigan, USA. His parents, Carl and Gloria Page, were both involved in the field of computer science, which greatly influenced his interest in technology and innovation.

Education

Page attended the University of Michigan, where he earned a degree in computer engineering. He later pursued a Ph.D. in computer science at Stanford University, where he met Sergey Brin, with whom he would later co-found Google.

Founding Google

In 1998, Larry Page and Sergey Brin founded Google while they were still Ph.D. students at Stanford. Their goal was to organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful. Google quickly became the world's leading search engine, revolutionizing the way people access information.

Achievements and Impact

Under Page’s leadership, Google expanded beyond search to create products like Gmail, Google Maps, and Android. He also played a crucial role in the creation of Alphabet Inc., Google’s parent company, to oversee its growing array of projects and subsidiaries.

Personal Life and Legacy

Aside from his contributions to technology, Page is known for his vision of advancing artificial intelligence, self-driving cars, and innovative technology solutions. His impact on the digital world continues to shape the future of technology.
